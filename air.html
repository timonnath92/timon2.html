AIR POLLUTION

Air pollution is the presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere, which can negatively impact human health, animals, plants, and the environment. These pollutants can be in the form of gases (such as carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides), particulates (such as dust, soot, and smoke), and liquids (like sulfuric acid). Air pollution is primarily caused by human activities like the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas) for energy and transportation, industrial emissions, agricultural activities (like livestock farming), deforestation, and waste incineration. It can also come from natural sources like wildfires, volcanic eruptions, and dust storms. Effects: Air pollution is linked to a variety of serious health issues, including respiratory diseases (e.g., asthma, bronchitis, and emphysema), heart diseases, and even cancer. Long-term exposure to polluted air can reduce life expectancy and lead to premature deaths. On a larger scale, it can contribute to environmental problems such as acid rain, smog, and the depletion of the ozone layer. It also accelerates global warming due to the emission of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane. Additionally, air pollution can damage crops, forests, and bodies of water, disrupting ecosystems.
